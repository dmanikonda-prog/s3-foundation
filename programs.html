Agnihotra

"अग्निहोत्र" — The Sacred Fire Ritual

Agnihotra is the most fundamental Vedic ritual — a precise fire ceremony performed at sunrise and sunset every single day at S3 Gaushala. Using dried cow dung, ghee from our Gir cows, and specific mantras, this ancient practice purifies the atmosphere and creates profound inner peace.

Scientific research has shown that Agnihotra smoke helps neutralize harmful pathogens in the environment. The ash has medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic preparations.

  • ✦Frequency: Twice daily — at exact sunrise and sunset
  • ✦Materials: Dried cow dung, pure ghee, rice, copper pyramid
  • ✦Duration: 20–30 minutes per session
  • ✦Open to: All are welcome to attend, no experience needed

Annual Grand Event

Laksha Chandi Maha Yagnam

"लक्ष चण्डी महायज्ञम्" — The Grand Navaratri Yagna

S3 Foundation's most spectacular event — the Laksha (100,000) Chandi Maha Yagnam is performed during Navaratri (October). This is an unprecedented Vedic undertaking in the Americas, bringing together hundreds of priests, thousands of devotees, and divine grace on a monumental scale.

The Yagnam spans 10 days of continuous ritual — day and night — in our 30,000 sq.ft Yagna Shala. All participants receive accommodation, meals, and the profound blessing of this rare ritual.

  • ✦100,000 Chandi recitations of Devi Mahatmyam
  • ✦10,000,000 Navakshari (9-syllable) Mantra Japam
  • ✦1,000 Chandi Homams with sacred fires
  • ✦1,000,000 Navakshari Mantra Homam offerings
  • ✦Duration: October 16–26 (10 Days), during Navaratri

Satsang & Discourses

"सत्संग" — The Company of Truth

Regular satsangs bring together seekers to explore the depth of Vedic wisdom through scriptural discourses, bhajans, kirtan, and guided discussion. Our visiting Gurus and Acharyas bring teachings from Bhagavad Gita, Upanishads, Ramayana, and practical dharmic wisdom for modern life.

  • ✦Monthly discourses on Bhagavad Gita & Upanishads
  • ✦Interactive Q&A with visiting Gurus and Acharyas
  • ✦Bhajans, kirtans, and devotional singing
  • ✦Prasad distribution after each satsang
